Pro Bono Clinics & Projects

PCBA member attorneys volunteer their time to provide free legal help to Pueblo County residents who need it most.

Upcoming Clinics
Monthly

Free Legal Advice Clinic — Family Law

Walk-in clinic providing brief legal advice on family law matters including divorce, custody, child support, and protection orders. No appointment needed.

📅 When: 3rd Saturday of each month, 10 AM – 2 PM
📍 Where: Rawlings Library, 100 E. Abriendo Ave, Pueblo
🌐 Language: Español disponible
Quarterly

Veterans Legal Assistance Clinic

Free legal help for veterans and active-duty military on issues including benefits appeals, discharge upgrades, housing, and family law matters.

📅 When: Quarterly — see Events Calendar
📍 Where: Southern Colorado Veterans Coalition
👤 Who: Veterans and active-duty military
Monthly

Senior Legal Services Clinic

Free legal consultations for residents age 60 and older. No income guidelines. Topics include estate planning, benefits, housing, and consumer issues.

📅 When: 2nd Tuesday of each month, 9 AM – 12 PM
📍 Where: Pueblo Area Agency on Aging
👤 Who: Age 60+, no income limit
Ongoing

Ask-A-Lawyer

Brief, informal consultations with volunteer PCBA attorneys. Get answers to general legal questions — not full legal representation, but enough to know your next step.

📅 When: Check Events Calendar for schedule
📍 Where: Various Pueblo locations
✅ No appointment needed · Walk-ins welcome
Annual

Small Business Legal Clinic

Free legal consultations for small business owners on contracts, employment law, licensing, and business formation. Hosted in partnership with the Pueblo Chamber of Commerce.

📅 When: Annually — see Events Calendar
📍 Where: Pueblo Chamber of Commerce
👤 Who: Small business owners, Pueblo County
Ongoing

Courthouse Self-Help Desk

PCBA attorneys rotate through the Pueblo Combined Courthouse to assist self-represented litigants with forms, procedures, and general guidance.

📅 When: Tuesdays & Thursdays, 8:30 AM – 12 PM
📍 Where: Pueblo Combined Courts, 501 N. Elizabeth St.
✅ No appointment needed

Who Can Use These Clinics?

Most PCBA pro bono clinics are open to any Pueblo County resident who needs free legal assistance. Some programs have specific eligibility requirements (such as income limits or veteran status) which are noted above.

Clinics provide brief advice and guidance — not ongoing legal representation. If your matter requires full representation, our volunteer attorneys can help connect you with Colorado Legal Services or other resources.

All consultations are confidential. You do not need to bring a lawyer to the clinic.

Volunteer as an Attorney

PCBA member attorneys are the heart of these programs. If you're a licensed attorney interested in volunteering at a clinic, contact the PCBA to be added to our volunteer roster.

Pro bono service earns CLE credit under Colorado Supreme Court Rule 260.5. Volunteer attorneys may receive up to 7 hours of CLE credit per year for pro bono service.

Partner Organizations

The PCBA co-sponsors pro bono projects with these organizations to expand access to justice in Pueblo County.

⚖️Colorado Legal Services — Free civil legal help for low-income Coloradans. Actual cases from PCBA clinics are often referred here for full representation.
📋Colorado Bar Association — The PCBA co-sponsors many pro bono projects with the CBA's Access to Justice initiatives statewide.
📚Rawlings Library — Community partner hosting monthly family law clinics in a welcoming, accessible public space in downtown Pueblo.
🎖️Southern Colorado Veterans Coalition — Partner for the quarterly Veterans Legal Assistance Clinic serving Pueblo's veteran community.
