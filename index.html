Bylaws of the Pueblo County Bar Association
Adopted November 10, 2015
Article I — Purpose & Object
The purpose of the Pueblo County Bar Association is to serve as a professional association for attorneys at law who reside in, or practice in, Pueblo County, Colorado — promoting justice, its administration and availability to all sectors of society.
Article II — Membership
Any person licensed to practice law by the Supreme Court of the State of Colorado shall be eligible to become a regular member. Membership may be terminated only by non-payment of dues or by disbarment or suspension by the Supreme Court of the State of Colorado.
Article VI — Dues
Annual dues are payable on or before the first day of July. The amount of dues for all classes of membership will be as decided by the membership from time to time. In the event of hardship, any Regular member may apply to the Board of Directors for a waiver of annual dues.
Article VII — Meetings
Monthly meetings shall be held on the second Tuesday of each month at a place to be designated by the Board of Directors. The annual meeting shall be held between May 15 and September 1 of each year.

Who We Are

The bar for Pueblo County

The Pueblo County Bar Association has served the legal community of southeastern Colorado for over a century. We bring together attorneys, judges, and legal professionals to strengthen the practice of law and expand access to justice for all residents.

We are a voluntary bar association — a professional home for Pueblo's legal community, not a licensing body. Our work spans professional development, community outreach, pro bono service, and advocacy for a fair and accessible court system.

Access to JusticeFree clinics and referrals for residents who need legal help but can't afford it.
Professional DevelopmentCLE courses and networking opportunities that keep Pueblo attorneys sharp.
Community LeadershipAdvocating for a well-functioning, independent judiciary in Pueblo County.
